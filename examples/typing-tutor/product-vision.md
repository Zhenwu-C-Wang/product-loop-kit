# Typing Tutor Product Vision

Created: 2026-06-29

## Target User

- Primary user: A young learner who is starting to build keyboard fluency.
- Secondary user: A parent or teacher who supervises short practice sessions.
- Situation where the product is used: The learner practices for a few minutes at home or in a classroom with an adult nearby.
- Current workaround: Generic typing websites, paper prompts, or adult-made practice lists that do not adapt to the learner's pace.

## Human Context Advantage

- The learner needs short, low-friction sessions and immediate feedback.
- The adult needs enough control to adjust difficulty without turning practice into setup work.
- Motivation matters as much as raw speed; the flow should feel calm and rewarding.
- Early versions should avoid accounts, leaderboards, and long-term setup.

## Product Bet

We believe that:

> For young learners practicing keyboard fluency, Typing Tutor will help them complete short daily sessions by combining simple prompts, immediate scoring, and adult-controlled difficulty, and we will know it is working when learners complete sessions without adult rescue and adults can choose the next difficulty quickly.

## Success Criteria

- Learner completes a short session and understands the result.
- Adult can change difficulty before the next session.
- Scoring handles common edge cases.
- The first session can be evaluated without accounts or persistence.

## Non-Goals

- User accounts.
- Long-term progress dashboards.
- Competitive leaderboards.
- Paid content packs.

## Product Principles

- Short sessions first.
- Adult control without adult workload.
- Feedback should be immediate and kind.
- Reward systems should not hide the typing task.

## Current Risks

| Risk | Why It Matters | How We Will Learn |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Difficulty may be too coarse. | Learners may get bored or frustrated. | Observe completion and adult adjustment behavior. |
| Scoring may feel confusing. | Learners need clear feedback to improve. | Review result screen with learner/adult pair. |
| Adult controls may interrupt practice. | The product should reduce supervision effort. | Time how long it takes to choose the next prompt length. |

## Feedback Sources

| Source | Cadence | Owner | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Observed practice session | After first usable prototype | Builder | Watch for confusion, hesitation, and adult intervention. |
| Adult review | After each prototype | Builder | Ask whether the settings are enough for the next session. |
| Session completion data | After basic instrumentation | Builder | Track completion, retries, and prompt difficulty. |
